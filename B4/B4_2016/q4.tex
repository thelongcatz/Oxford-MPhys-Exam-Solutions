\begin{parts}
	\part 1st gen in SM:
	
	Quarks: $\Qup$, $\Qdown$
	
	Leptons: $\electron$, $\mu_e$
	
	So $Z$ decays:
	
	$\Zz \to \Qup \bar{\Qup}$, $\Zz \to \Qdown \bar{\Qdown}$, $\Zz \to \electron \aneutrinoe$, $\Zz \to \positron \neutrinoe$
	
	$W$ decays:
	
	$\Wn \to \bar{\Qup} \Qdown$, $W^- \to \Qup \bar{\Qdown}$, $\Wn \to \electron \aneutrinoe$, $\Wn \to \positron \neutrinoe$
	
	For 1st gen, all particles have masses $\ll m_W c^2$, so 4-point interaction is a good approximation $\Rightarrow$ Sargent's rule applies and d.o.s. $\propto Q^5$.
	
	So:
	\begin{align*}
		\textnormal{BR}(W \to l \nu_l) &= \frac{m_e^5}{m_e^5 + 3m_n^5} = \num{8.29e-12} \\
		\textnormal{BR}(W \to q q^\prime) &= \frac{3m_u^5}{m_e^5 + 3m_u^5} = 1
	\end{align*}
	
	\part For each generation, we have degeneracy of 3 from colours + 1 from leptons so:
	\begin{align*}
		4n &= \frac{2085}{232} \mtext{assuming similar partial widths} \\
		n &\simeq \num{2.25}
	\end{align*}
	So there must be at least 2 generations of particles.
	
	\part For 4th gen to occur, their masses must be so massive ($\sim m_W c^2$) so that BR is negligible.
\end{parts}
